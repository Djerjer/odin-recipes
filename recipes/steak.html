Sirloin Steak Recipe (Pan-Fry)

Here we have what looks to be a properly cooked sirloin steak resting beautifully on a cutting board, accompanied by some ingredients. Below I will go over what it takes to make this simple, yet delicious recipe that will cause you to become the next Cole Train.

Ingredients:

  • Sirloin Steak
  • Peppercorn
  • Fresh garlic and/or garlic powder
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Rosemary
  • Thyme
  • Cooking oil

Steak Temps:

  • Extra Rare/Blue: 115F-120F
  • Rare: 125F-130F
  • Medium-Rare: 130F-140F
  • Medium: 140F-150F
  • Medium-Well: 150F-155F
  • Well-Done: 160F+

Steps:

  1. Season up the steak.
  2. Add your cooking oil into your choice of pan (Medium-high heat).
  3. Place steak into the pan after the pan has heated up.
  4. Cook the steak on each side for about 2 minutes to create a nice crust (Very important step. When it comes to cooking meat, a lot of flavor comes from creating a nice crust on the outside).
  5. After each side has been cooked to your liking, go ahead and add in your butter (at this point, you may turn the heat down a bit).
  6. Add in your sprigs of thyme, rosemary, and whole garlic(s) (you're going to be basting the steak until the inside is about 145F for the inside to be medium-rare).
  7. Take the steak off the pan and have it rest on a cutting board for about 10 minutes (the steak will actually continue to slightly cook once it's freshly off of the pan).
